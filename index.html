Designing eye-catching gameplay
description
Given its small marketing budget, Super Mombo Quest had to stand out and bring in players through its looks and feel alone. We focused on creating fluid, responsive controls and eye-catching visual effects that would make the game instantly recognizable in crowded storefronts. Every animation was hand-tuned to maximize juice and player satisfaction, from screen-shake on impacts to particle bursts on collectibles. The color palette was carefully chosen to pop on both mobile screens and large monitors, ensuring thumbnails and screenshots grabbed attention at any size. We iterated on dozens of visual prototypes before settling on the final style that balanced readability with flair.

title
Crafting a monetization-driven<br>
videoInfoHeight

content
Mombo Combo Legacy's predecessor quickly reached over a million players on mobile. However, revenue there was underwhelming, as it had few retention mechanics and relied on interstitials for monetization. Seeing the potential, our team decided to develop a sequel - Mombo Combo Legacy - now planned as a F2P experience first and foremost. This meant rethinking almost every system from the ground up: session length, reward pacing, difficulty curves, gacha mechanics, battle passes, and daily login incentives all had to be designed to maximize long-term engagement without alienating the core fanbase. We conducted extensive competitor analysis across dozens of successful F2P mobile titles to identify patterns that resonated with players. The result was a layered economy with soft and hard currencies, a seasonal content calendar, and a progression system that rewarded both daily play and weekend deep-dives.

Crafting a more robust metagame to
description
When it came to monetization, I noticed two issues with the previous title. The first was players tended toward long, multi-hour sessions, finishing the game in a few days rather than making it become a sustained daily habit. Without recurring engagement, ad impressions and in-app purchase opportunities were limited. The second issue was that the premium currency had minimal sink diversity — players could earn everything they wanted quickly, leaving no aspirational goals. To address this, I redesigned the session flow to encourage shorter but more frequent play sessions with cooldown timers and energy mechanics. I also introduced a crafting system that served as a deep currency sink, giving long-term players meaningful goals while providing natural monetization touchpoints through premium materials and time-skip options.
